NU Map

A degree audit that plans forward, looks back, and makes prerequisite chains visible at a glance.

The audit checks requirements. This helps you plan around them.

Northeastern's degree audit is effective at answering whether you're on track to graduate. This project explores a different question: what's the best way to get there? It puts the course planner at the center and gives students tools to sequence courses, visualize dependencies, and experiment with plans quickly.

The planner is the primary view.

The course planner takes the majority of the screen. The course bank and graduation panel are accessible in compact side panels — present when needed, out of the way when not.

Each semester displays courses with 3 or more credits in a clean row. Smaller credits are accessible below, keeping the default view focused on what matters most.

The current audit allocates most of its space to the requirements list, with the planner as a narrower secondary panel.

Prerequisites rendered as arrows, not lists.

Clicking a course draws SVG arrows to its direct prerequisites and dependents. The visualization is scoped — only immediate parents and children appear, keeping things readable.

When a prerequisite is placed in the wrong order, a red arrow marks the conflict. When a prerequisite isn't placed at all, a separate warning appears.

Prerequisite information is currently available as text. Tracing multi-step chains across semesters requires checking each course individually.

Drag to place. Drag back to remove. Undo everything.

Courses move between the course bank and the planner by dragging. Removing a course works the same way in reverse. Undo and redo are mapped to standard keyboard shortcuts — making it easy to experiment without consequence.

The current process for adding and removing courses involves more steps, and there is no undo support.

Plans that include where you've been, not just where you're going.

Plans begin from your entry semester. Courses you've already completed sit alongside courses you're planning, giving you the full picture in one view.

Currently, new plans start from the current semester. There is no way to view previously completed coursework within the planner.

Additional details.

Availability alerts

Flags courses that likely aren't offered in a given semester. Users can override the alert if they have better information.

Color-coded courses

Courses are colored by department code, making it easy to scan the distribution across semesters.

Course starring

Bookmark courses you're considering for quick reference without committing them to a semester.

Save and load

Plans export as JSON files and can be loaded back at any time. No data is lost between sessions.

PDF export

Generate a formatted PDF for advisor meetings, personal records, or sharing with peers.

Cohort switching

Change cohorts to see how your plan maps to different requirement sets without starting over.

Light and dark mode

Full theme support across the interface.

AI-ready summary

Generate a structured text summary of your plan for use with a chatbot or AI advisor.

Placed-out courses

Mark courses you've placed out of so they're reflected in your plan and progress tracking.
